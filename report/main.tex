\chapter{Introduction}
This report summarizes the current status of transient grating signal analysis.
It is intended as the companion document for the code and notebook workflow in this project.

\chapter{Fitting Models}

For this report, three different models have been implemented and fitted to the data. The models are described in the following sections.

\section{Model 1}

The first model is described by the following equation:
\begin{align}
    \begin{split}
        f(t)
        &= \qty[A_1 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau} + A_2]
        \circledast \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} e^{-(t-t_0)^2/(2\sigma^2)} \\
        &= A_1 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau} e^{\sigma^2/(2\tau^2)}
        \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0-\sigma^2/\tau}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right) \\
        &\quad + A_2 \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right)
    \end{split}
\end{align}

\section{Model 2}
The second model is described by the following equation:
\begin{align}
    \begin{split}
    f(t)
    &= \qty[A_1 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau} + A_2 + A_3 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_2}\sin(\omega t-\phi)]
    \circledast \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} e^{-(t-t_0)^2/(2\sigma^2)} \\
    &= A_1 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau} e^{\sigma^2/(2\tau^2)}
    \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0-\sigma^2/\tau}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right) \\
    &\quad + A_2 \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right) \\
    &\quad + A_3 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_2} e^{\sigma^2/(2\tau_2^2)}
    \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0-\sigma^2/\tau_2}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right)
    (\sin(\omega t-\phi)-\cos(\omega t-\phi)).
    \end{split}
    \end{align}

\section{Model 3}
The third model is described by the following equation:
\begin{align}
    \begin{split}
        f(t) &= \qty[A_1 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_1} + A_2 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_2} + A_3 (e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_{20}} - e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_{10}}) + A_4]\sin(\omega t-\phi) \\
        &\quad \circledast \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} e^{-(t-t_0)^2/(2\sigma^2)} \\
        &= A_1 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_1} e^{\sigma^2/(2\tau_1^2)}
        \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0-\sigma^2/\tau_1}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right) \\
        &\quad + A_2 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_2} e^{\sigma^2/(2\tau_2^2)}
        \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0-\sigma^2/\tau_2}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right) \\
        &\quad + A_3 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_{20}} e^{\sigma^2/(2\tau_{20}^2)}
        \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0-\sigma^2/\tau_{20}}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right)[\sin(\omega t-\phi)-\cos(\omega t-\phi)] \\
        &\quad - A_3 e^{-(t-t_0)/\tau_{10}} e^{\sigma^2/(2\tau_{10}^2)}
        \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0-\sigma^2/\tau_{10}}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right)[\sin(\omega t-\phi)-\cos(\omega t-\phi)] \\
        &\quad + A_4 \left(1 + \erf\!\left(\frac{t-t_0}{\sqrt{2}\sigma}\right)\right),
    \end{split}
\end{align}
where $\frac{1}{\tau_{20}} = \frac{1}{\tau_2} + \frac{1}{t_{0}}$ and $\frac{1}{\tau_{10}} = \frac{1}{\tau_1} + \frac{1}{t_{0}}$.

\section{Observations}

\subsection{Constant Intensity Results}

It is observed that all models can fit the data well. However, Model 1 leads to a $\chi^2$/dof goodness of fit closer to 1 than Model 2 and Model 3. However, Model 2 and Model 3 can fit the data better than Model 1 since the value of $R^2$ is closer to 1 for Model 2 and Model 3. Also, by observing the residuals, it is observed that Model 2 and Model 3 are able to fit the data better than Model 1, since the residuals are smaller for Model 2 and Model 3.

\subsection{Constant Energy Results}

Similar to the constant intensity results, all models can fit the data well. However, Model 1 leads to a $\chi^2$/dof goodness of fit closer to 1 than Model 2 and Model 3. However, Model 2 and Model 3 (except for intensity values of 3.7 $\upmu$J and 3.9 $\upmu$J) can fit the data better than Model 1 since the value of $R^2$ is closer to 1 for Model 2 and Model 3. Also, by observing the residuals, it is observed that Model 2 and Model 3 are able to fit the data better than Model 1, since the residuals are smaller for Model 2 and Model 3.

Nevertheless, Models 2 and 3 lead to $\tau$ vs intensity plots that do not show a clear trend, while Model 1 does.

\subsection{Comparison of Models Results}

At a constant intensity of 2 $\upmu$J, Models 1 to 3 show $\tau$ vs energy plots that show a similar trend. However, the values of $\tau$ for Models 2 and 3 are consistently lower than the values of $\tau$ for Model 1. Also, for Models 2 and 3, error bars are noticeably bigger than the ones obtained for Model 1.

At a constant energy of 63.6 eV, only Model 1 shows a clear trend in the $\tau$ vs intensity plot while keeping error bars small. Models 2 and 3 lead to $\tau$ vs intensity plots that do not show a clear trend and keep error bars big.
